# Founder Response — Question Design & Decision Signal (v1)

## What draws me to YES/NO
YES/NO is built on a simple truth: people are often blocked less by missing information and more by signal overload.

Most systems ask people to explain more. YES/NO asks people to decide more clearly.

## Which decisions are most buried in noise
The noisiest decisions are usually identity-and-direction decisions:
- stay vs leave
- ship vs delay
- call vs avoid
- commit vs keep options open

These are rarely pure knowledge problems. They are clarity-under-pressure problems.

## What happens around question 8–12
After a sequence of clean binary prompts, many users shift from narrative mode to recognition mode.

Instead of endlessly explaining, they begin noticing:
- where they are internally consistent
- where fear and desire conflict
- where their own answer was already present but buried

Compared to long prompts or multi-page forms, this usually produces:
- less cognitive theater
- faster clarity
- stronger commitment signal

## “The system does not decide for you. It learns how you decide.”
What the system learns is not primarily world facts. It learns decision behavior:
- preference stability vs volatility
- conflict patterns under uncertainty
- confidence progression across a question chain
- flip thresholds (when an answer changes)

So the value is behavioral understanding, not oracle prediction.

## What makes a good YES/NO question
A good question is:
- binary-clean (no hidden third state)
- emotionally legible
- temporally specific (now vs abstract)
- action-linked (if YES, then what?)
- contrastive enough to surface tension

A weak question collects data.
A strong question reveals signal.

## Most alive vs unresolved areas right now
Most alive:
- philosophy and layered architecture coherence

Most unresolved:
- deterministic question quality at scale

The key question:
How do we generate deterministic prompts that feel psychologically precise without drifting into randomness or pseudo-therapy?

## Closing
People often already know the answer.
The signal is buried under noise.

YES/NO is designed to surface that signal one binary step at a time.
