# First Pilot Briefing Note v0 — Presenter

## what to say in 30 seconds
“YES/NO is a radically simple AI interaction model: instead of writing prompts, the system asks one question at a time and the user answers only YES or NO. We’re testing whether this feels clearer and more natural than prompt-heavy interfaces.”

## what to show
- one clean session flow (question → question → question → artifact → completion)
- deterministic path with explicit YES/NO decisions
- minimal interface behavior and clear completion state

## what not to promise
- do not imply production readiness
- do not present roadmap items as shipped
- do not promise timeline/feature commitments in-session
- do not broaden sharing scope beyond controlled pilot

## what questions to ask after demo
- “What did you think this product was in the first 30 seconds?”
- “Was the YES/NO interaction clear?”
- “What felt most powerful?”
- “What felt confusing or unfinished?”
- “Would you want to try this again?”

## how to close the conversation
- thank them for early-stage feedback
- restate prototype status and current constraints
- confirm you’ll synthesize their feedback with other pilot signals before making changes
